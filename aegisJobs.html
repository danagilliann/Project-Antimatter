PhD Student Position at The University of Bern, Switzerland

Available immediately

A position is available immediately at the Albert Einstein Center for Fundamental Physics (Laboratory for High Energy Physics) to perform a PhD thesis on the AEgIS experiment on antihydrogen gravity.

Job specifications:

  • Maintenance, operation and data evaluation of the scintillating fiber annihilation detector (FACT) that monitors the formation of the antihydrogen cloud.
  • Conceptual design and preparation of the first attempts to measure the gravitational force on antihydrogen (thesis subject).
The position requires frequent trips to CERN.

Research Fellowship

Deadline: 2 March, 2015

If you’re a recent PhD holder, you’re no doubt looking for the chance to make your mark. Here it is: you could spend up to three years working right at the forefront of scientific research as a CERN research fellow. While most fellows at CERN work in particle physics, CERN has a large breadth of activities and the AEgIS experiment has opportunities for physicists from a wide range of fields: lasers, atomic physics, positronium, ultra-cold plasmas, ... Furthermore, if you are interested in working with us, we can help you prepare a submission to the Horizon 2020 individual fellowships supported by the EU.

The eligibility and employment conditions for CERN fellowships are given here. For more information please contact Michael Doser.

CERN doctoral student program

Deadline: 28 April, 2015

CERN's doctoral student program is a chance to work on your thesis while spending 6 to 36 months at the forefront of science. This program is tailored for students specializing in applied physics, or whose thesis contains a dominant component in this direction.

The eligibility and employment conditions are given here. For more information please contact Michael Doser.

CERN technical student program

Deadline: 28 April, 2015

There’s no better way to learn than on-the-job. When that job happens to be in a world-famous organisation and centre of scientific excellence, even better. If you’re an undergraduate in Applied Physics or a related field and are looking for a practical training period or a place to complete your final project (a number of students have completed their master thesis with AEgIS in the last 2 years), you could spend 4 to 12 months at CERN during the course of your studies.

The eligibility and employment conditions are given here. For more information please contact Michael Doser.
